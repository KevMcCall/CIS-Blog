Top 3 Most Dangerous Threats to Personal Computers

With the constant changes and upgrages to computers, and technology, cyber criminals are always looking to take advantage of your computer security and vulnerablities. The first step in protecting your computer against most cyber threats is being aware of different types of vulnerabilites that could put you at risk, securing your PC against those attacks, and knowing what to look for. The 3 biggest threats to personal computers from my experience are:

  • Lack of Anti-Virus/Firewall
  • Phishing
  • User Error

While so many people are now online and using computers (especially at home) more than before, the majority of users are still unaware of the potential threats and how to secure their computers. The majority of computers bought from stores come pre-installed with Antivirus software and are already protecting the user as soon as they start using their computer. However these programs do have a trial period and once they run out, uers will have to setup some type of antivirus software. The fact is many people do not do this or simply don't know how and leave their computer setup for attacks and vulnerablities, such as viruses, which can cause all sorts of problems for the user. That's why it's very important to always keep your antivirus software up to date and make sure your firewall is turned on, to block potential threats.

Phishing is another big threat to users and their information. I believe it's important to be able to spot fake websites, emails, and other programs that are build for the purpose of stealing your information and potentially, your identity. Phishing is when hackers setup websites, emails, and programs to mimic the original site as to throw off the user. For example, a hacker might duplicate a website to look exactly like your bank website or social media account. Everything looks legit from first glance, but if you were to enter in your information, instead of it being sent to the correct server and logging you in, your username/password, credit card number, and address, would then be sent to the hacker. This gives them everything they need to take over your account and make your life harder. That's why I would suggest always looking at the URL of the website you are on and making sure it's the correct website address, instead of a dummy URL, created to steal information.

As I had mentioned before, technology is more used today than in previous years, however, even with all of the information and software available to protect us, people do make mistakes. This is what's called user error. People are hacked all the time and the majority of those attacks could be prevented if the user took the necessary steps to protect themselves and knew what to look for and what to stay away from. If I could suggest anything, I would strongly suggest people to always keep an antivirus on their computer, keep it updated, and turn on your firewall to block incoming threats. Don't click on links that look "phishy" or any offers, or emails that sound too good to be true. Always remember, companies will never ask for your username or password. So if someone who is claiming to be from a certain company with which you have an account with and is asking for your information, you can bet they are trying to take advantage of you.

Introduction To Networking

What's a Network?
What Is It? What Does It Do? Examples Terms & Vocab Visual
A computer network connects nodes, like computers, routers, and switches, using cables or wireless signals. These connections allow the devices in a network to communicate and share information with one another. LAN: Local Area Network WAN: Wide Area Network Desktop computers, laptops, cellphones, tablets, mainframes, and servers.
Your ISP (Internet Service Provider) which is also a service provider network.
  • LAN
  • WAN
  • Node
  • Switch
  • NIC
  • ISP


What Are Protocols?
What Are They? What Do They Do? Examples Terms & Vocab Visual
Protocols are sets of rules that control the communications between computers on a network. In order for two or more devices on a network to talk to each other and share data, they must be speaking the same langauge. Protocols are required to ensure that your computer can communicate with another computer within a network. HTTP/HTTPs is a protocol which is used to access websites on the Internet. FTP protocol is used to transfer files between 2 or more devices. Email protocols such as POP3, IMAP, and SMTP are used to send email to other devices in a network.
  • HTTP
  • HTTPs
  • POP3/IMAP/SMTP
  • TCP/IP
  • FTP


What Are Protocol Layers?
What Are They? What Do They Do? Examples Terms & Vocab Visual
Most network protocols are structured as a series of layers, referred to collectively as Open Systems Interconnection (OSI) Each layer is designed for a specific purpose. Each layer exists on both the sending and receiving hosts. A specific layer on one machine sends or receives exactly the same object that another machine's peer process sends or receives. The physical layer defines the means of transmitting raw bits over a physical data link connecting network nodes and the Data Link handles things like Ethernet, Switch, and Bridge.
  • OSI
  • Physical
  • Data Link
  • Network
  • Transport
  • Session
  • Presentation
  • Application


What Is Network Interconnection/Internet?
What Is It? What Does It Do? Examples Terms & Vocab Visual
Network Interconnection is the mutual exchange of traffic across separate networks between different Internet Service Provider's (ISP's). Joins many networks into a single system that permits one connected device to reach other connected devices, regardless of where or how they are connected. An example of Network Interconnection is users requesting data on the internet such as a website, image, song, or other data. Two ISP's sending and receiving data from one another, then provide that data to it's users. All of these processes are made possible by interconnection, which connects all different types of devices to one another in order to send/recieve information.
  • NIC
  • ISP
  • MINs - Multistage Interconnection Networks

How The Internet Works

  1. In your own words, what is a network?
    A network is two or more devices connected to each other wirelessly or through use of cables, such as ethernet.
  2. What’s the difference between the web and the internet?
    The internet is a global network and the web (www) is a collection of information that is accessed by the internet.
  3. The internet is not a physical place or chip, so how would you explain it?
    The internet is not a physical place or chip, but more of a large connection of networks and servers connected to each other to share information.
  4. How is ISP related to the internet?
    Your ISP or Internet Service Provider is what provides you access to the internet.
  5. Why was the internet created, when?
    The internet was created back in the 1980’s and from my understanding was originally created as a military experiment.
  6. What is a DNS and why is it necessary?
    A DNS is a Domain Name System and provides domain name for websites/webpages on the internet.
  7. How has the internet expanded in the last 10 years in relation to computers?
    In the last 10 years the internet has become much more accessible to everyone, going from computers, to now being able to be accessed by phones, tablets, etc.

Computer Shoes

Imagine that you're living in the year 2090. Technology has advanced to the point that any inanimate object can function as a computer. Now... Imagine that technology, in your shoes.

Tech Features:

  • Input: The shoes feature a small LCD display which can be used to change the color of the shoes, loosen or tighten the shoes to your preference, display exercise stats, as well as many other settings. It also features a Micro USB port to charge them.

  • Memory: The shoe can record for 30 days worth of data. Every new month is a fresh start. Throughout the month the runner can access all the stored data in a Monthly Statistics tab.

  • Output: Data can be synced to your profile via an app or using the LCD display. They can also play music through small integrated speakers, which are built into the shoes.

  • Control Unit and Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU): Both are located in the shoes left sole. The control unit tells the ALU what operation to perform based on the data the wearer enters and the ALU then stores the result in an output register.
